About the Old World Atlas

The Atlas of the Old World is a fan project dedicated to mapping the world of Warhammer Fantasy. This project attempts to create, as much as is possible, an accurate and consistent representation of the Warhammer world, with gaps in the lore generously filled with my own interpretations or the interpretations of others in the community. No AI is used in any part of the creative process; topographic maps, rivers, settlement names and positions are all hand-drawn/-written/-placed. The front-end Javascript used for displaying the project in the form of a webpage was assisted in part by AI.

Credit and Thanks

This project would never have been possible without the tremendous creative and technical efforts of others in the community.

Andy Law

Andy Law has been involved with WFRP since its 2nd Edition, and was a key writer, game designer, artist and cartographer for the game's 4th Edition. He is actively involved in LAWHammer, an on-going WFRP 4e actual-play. Join the LAWHammer Discord. Many parts of this map are derived from, in particular, Andy's detailed maps of the Reikland, Middenland, and Middenheim, in addition to other maps of his, both published and unpublished. Content like this, and more, can be found on the LAWhammer Patreon.

Alfred Nuñez Jr. (aka MadAlfred)

Alfred Nuñez Jr. (aka MadAlfred) has been creating maps, gazetteers, and personal projects related to the Old World for over twenty-five years. His work, both published and fan-made (which has often snuck its way into the canon anyway), has been a source of inspiration and reference for this project. Check out his work at MadAlfred. I thank him personally for sharing his extensive knowledge and resources with me during this project.

Please note that there are conflicting and inconsistent sources for the locations and populations of many sites in the Warhammer world. The following 'canonicity tier list' was approximately used to prioritize sources when discrepancies arose. This is a personal choice and may differ from other interpretations, or be applied inconsistently in some cases:
  1. Recent Games Workshop publications (Warhammer Fantasy Roleplay 4e, Warhammer: The Old World material)
  2. Maps and gazetteers by Andy Law, both published and unpublished
  3. Older Games Workshop publications (WFRP 1e, WFB army books pre-8th edition)
  4. Other fan interpretations and resources (MadAlfred, community maps, etc.)
